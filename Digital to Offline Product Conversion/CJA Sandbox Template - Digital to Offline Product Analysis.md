## 📌 Description

A comprehensive **cross-channel product analysis template** designed to:

- Measure how digital product engagement drives offline purchases
- Identify high- and low-converting products
- Compare online vs offline purchase behavior
- Analyze time-to-conversion using cohort analysis
- Provide a structured framework for omnichannel insights

## 📊 Key Panels & Insights

### 1. Cross-Channel Conversion Overview (KPIs)

- Digital Product Views: ~150K
- Offline Orders: ~32K
- Digital → Offline Conversion Rate: ~21%

👉 Establishes overall relationship between digital engagement and offline purchasing

### 2. Digital to Offline Conversion by Product

- Product-level:
    - Views
    - Offline Orders
    - Conversion Rate

👉 Identifies which products drive offline conversion and purchase intent

### 3. Channel Preference (Online vs Offline)

- Top Products Purchased Online
- Top Products Purchased Offline
- Revenue + Order comparison

👉 Reveals product-level channel preference and differences in purchase behavior

### 4. Daily Cohort (Last 14 Days)

- Time to offline purchase after product view
- Day-by-day conversion behavior

👉 Highlights short-term decision window and near-term conversion patterns

### 5. Weekly Cohort (Last 13 Weeks)

- Longer-term conversion trends
- Extended purchase behavior

👉 Shows how long digital influence persists over time

## 🔍 Key Observations

- Digital engagement is a strong driver of offline purchases (~21% conversion)
- Significant variation in conversion rates by product
- Clear differences between:
    - High-intent products (high conversion)
    - Browsing-heavy products (low conversion)
- Channel preference varies by product:
    - Some are digital-first
    - Others are store-driven
- Most conversions occur within a short time window after product engagement

## 🚧 Opportunities for Enhancement

- Add **Channel Mix %** (Online vs Offline per product)

- Add **segmentation** (device, new vs returning users)
- Incorporate **funnel visualization** for deeper path analysis

## 🧠 Pattern / Design Insight

This template follows a strong analytical progression:

1. **Top-line KPIs (What is happening)**
2. **Product-level drivers (What is driving it)**
3. **Channel behavior (Where it happens)**
4. **Timing analysis (When it happens)**

👉 Represents a **complete omnichannel behavioral analysis framework**
